    SA Rolex is one of the world’s most prestigious luxury watch brands, headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland. The company was founded in 1905 in London by Hans Wilsdorf and Alfred Davis before relocating to Switzerland in 1919. Rolex is renowned for its high-quality craftsmanship, luxurious materials, and exceptional precision. Key Facts About Rolex: 1. Innovations & Technologies: • First Waterproof Wristwatch: Rolex introduced the Oyster in 1926, the world’s first waterproof wristwatch. • First Self-Winding Automatic Watch: In 1931, Rolex developed the Perpetual movement, setting the standard for modern automatic watches. • Professional Diver’s Watch: The Rolex Submariner, introduced in 1953, became one of the most iconic diving watches, water-resistant up to 300 meters. • Watch for Explorers & Pilots: Rolex developed the Explorer and GMT-Master models, designed for adventurers and aviators. 2. Most Iconic Rolex Models: • Submariner – A legendary diver’s watch with 300m water resistance. • Daytona – A chronograph watch designed for racing drivers. • Datejust – One of the first watches to feature an automatic date function. • GMT-Master II – Designed for pilots, featuring a dual time zone function. • Yacht-Master – A nautical-inspired luxury sports watch. 3. Presence in Sports & Luxury: • Rolex sponsors prestigious events, including Wimbledon, the US Open (tennis), Formula 1, and Le Mans 24 (motorsport). • Rolex watches are a symbol of success, often worn by celebrities, business leaders, and politicians. 4. Independence & Quality: • Rolex is a privately held company, owned by the Hans Wilsdorf Foundation, which allows it to focus on long-term quality rather than short-term profits. • Every Rolex watch is crafted with premium materials, including 18k gold, titanium, ceramic, and 904L stainless steel. Quick Facts About Rolex: • Produces around 800,000 to 1 million watches annually. • Each watch undergoes rigorous testing to ensure durability and precision. • The Rolex crown logo symbolizes excellence and prestige. Rolex is more than just a watch brand—it’s a symbol of luxury, craftsmanship, and timeless innovation!
